DrivenSys

Traffic congestion in urban areas remains one of the most pressing challenges for city planners, commuters, and the transportation ecosystem. Accurate and real-time spatio-temporal traffic prediction is essential for the development of effective traffic management strategies and intelligent transportation systems. This study introduces a novel Convolutional Temporal Neural Network System (CTNS) designed to predict traffic flow in urban areas by capturing spatial and temporal dependencies in traffic patterns. CTNS combines convolutional layers to model spatial traffic patterns across multiple city regions with recurrent neural network components to capture the temporal dynamics of traffic flow over time. This architecture enables the system to effectively analyze complex interactions between traffic variables, ensuring accurate and reliable predictions. The CTNS model integrates diverse datasets, including real-time traffic updates, historical traffic patterns, and external factors such as road conditions, weather, and public events. This holistic approach significantly enhances the model’s predictive accuracy and adaptability, allowing it to provide location-specific and time-specific traffic congestion forecasts. By understanding these patterns, city planners can optimize traffic signals, reroute vehicles, and manage congestion proactively. To augment its functionality, the system includes an innovative SOS mechanism powered by GPS and GSM modules. Drivers can activate this feature via a push-button interface during emergencies or uneasy situations. Once triggered, the system sends real-time alerts containing the driver’s precise location to relevant security systems, healthcare services, and traffic management authorities. This ensures a swift and targeted response to emergencies, potentially saving lives and improving road safety. The CTNS architecture is designed to address the inherent challenges of urban traffic, such as its dynamic and nonlinear nature. By effectively managing sudden traffic fluctuations and long-term congestion trends, the model demonstrates exceptional robustness and scalability. Experimental results, derived from data collected in a major urban center, reveal that CTNS outperforms traditional statistical and machine learning methods in terms of accuracy, scalability, and efficiency. The proposed system not only contributes to reducing traffic congestion and improving mobility but also enhances driver safety and supports smart city initiatives. By integrating advanced predictive modeling with emergency response capabilities, CTNS presents a comprehensive solution for modern urban traffic management.
